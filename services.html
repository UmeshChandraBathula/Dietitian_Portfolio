What's the difference between a Dietitian and a Nutritionist? +
In India, the title "nutritionist" is currently unregulated — there is no single national body that licenses or registers nutritionists, so the title alone tells you little. This is changing: the National Commission for Allied and Healthcare Professions (NCAHP) Act, 2021 is gradually bringing allied health professions, including nutrition, under a structured regulatory framework. A "Registered Dietitian" in India typically refers to someone with a formal dietetics degree (B.Sc. or M.Sc. Dietetics) registered with a state-level body. Niharika holds a Diploma in Food Therapy & Nutrition from the National Board of Vocational Training Education (NBVTE), along with a Master of Pharmacy — a combination that gives her both the clinical nutrition training and the deep pharmacological background to provide well-rounded, science-grounded dietary guidance.
Do you offer telehealth appointments? +
Yes! All services are available online, and I work with clients across India and internationally. Sessions are conducted via a secure video call platform. All you need is a device with a camera and a quiet space.
How many sessions will I need? +
It varies by person and goal. Some clients see meaningful progress in 4-6 sessions; others prefer ongoing support. We always start with an initial consultation to understand your needs and then discuss the best path forward — with absolutely no pressure to commit to anything upfront.
Will you give me a meal plan? +
Yes, if that's what works best for you! Some clients love a detailed meal plan, others prefer flexible frameworks and principles. We tailor the tools to your learning style and lifestyle — not a one-size-fits-all approach.
